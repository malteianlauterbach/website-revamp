In a recent conversation with Zach Latta, the founder of Hack Club, I said something I'd like to repeat here. I believe that, if we give students from all over the world a base to meet each other, have them oversee their differences, the pressure and hate that is implanted by their peers, the general consesus of their sub civilization, i think we can influence how we, as humans, as one generation, as one civilization see each other, how teens see teens from other countrys. I believe that the internet is similar to that, if used correctly. Any leader with an high impact on a community that is truly international , who uses the tools given to him (his influence) will be able to establish understanding and change. I believe that we, you and I, my dear reader, as the ones with the ability to engage users will and should be the ones to do it. Better today than tomorrow.
