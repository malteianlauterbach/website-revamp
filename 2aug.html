On a global level, we suck at scaling

Everyone who has ever founded a startup or workedy in one knows one essential part of making a profit: A startup lives because it's features, it's pricing, it's architecture, its market readiness are scaled to the expectations of its users and the numbers of users. If the leader fails to scale correctly, he will certainly lose users and another company will emerge on the ruins of his company. That's how the market works. Bad scaling=Bad leadership=No win But how does that work on a global scale? What happens if a whole country fails to scale to the expectations of its users? the answer is simple, we currently see it I everywhere in the world In America, Hong Kong, Bolivia, Iraq, India, France and more. The users (the population) riots or even coups to show their disagreement with their leaders, how they reign, how they scale. This pandemic brings forth the the worst parts of our civilization, shows us, how we, globally failed at scaling. To make a change in the future, we need to comprehend how we should scale a country correctly. As soon as possible.
